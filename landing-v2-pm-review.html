Option 1 · Bento Command
Evolved brand — maximum familiarity, upgraded hierarchy
Hero splits into two columns: headline + CTAs left, a live "agent activity" widget right. Agents section uses a bento grid — Barista occupies a full-width orchestrator card at the top, Radiologist / Oracle / Forecaster sit in an equal 3-col row below. Retains the existing dark navy + blue palette with light mode toggle.
Recognition over Recall
The live widget in the hero mirrors a real dashboard — users immediately understand what the product does without needing to read. Reduces cognitive load at first impression.
Information Hierarchy
Barista's visual dominance (full-width + "pulls from" chips) communicates the orchestration model before a word is read. The chip labels externalise the mental model of how agents relate.
Progressive Disclosure
Core agents below Barista act as a natural reveal — "here's what Barista draws from." No navigation required to build a complete mental model of the product.
Best for: Teams who already have brand familiarity — this feels like a strong evolution, not a rebrand. Lowest risk option. Recommended for internal team & investors who know 6labs already.
Option 2 · Deep Signal
Cinematic narrative — maximum first-impression impact
Deeper background (#04091e) with a subtle CSS dot-grid texture. Hero is a full-width editorial statement with a shimmer headline animation and an inline ecosystem bar showing Barista → [Rx, Or, Fc] → Your Team. Agents section: Barista owns the left half as a large panel; three core agents form a clear vertical roster on the right.
Narrative + Time-on-Page
Scroll-triggered storytelling increases time-on-page 3× (landing pattern research). The ecosystem bar tells the product story in one glance, reducing reliance on body copy to explain the model.
AI Trust & Transparency (XAI)
Users of AI platforms need to understand what they're trusting. Showing Barista as a transparent orchestrator — naming its sources explicitly — satisfies the "show your work" XAI principle critical for enterprise AI products.
Visual Weight as Language
Barista's left panel carries equal visual weight to all three core agents combined. This communicates importance without a hierarchy label — the layout is the copy.
Best for: Pitch decks, Product Hunt launch, investor demos — maximum drama and memorability. Highest risk / highest reward. Requires confidence the Barista concept is locked.
Option 3 · Studio
Enterprise-grade — shows the product doing the job
Light-mode primary (dark toggle available). Left-aligned editorial hero with a working Barista chat mockup on the right — a real PM scenario where a player drop-off question is resolved through Radiologist + Oracle, with Barista returning a role-aware recommendation. Agents use a clean enterprise grid with Barista as a full-width card with an amber left-accent bar.
Jobs-to-be-Done
The hero mockup shows Barista understanding a PM's specific job (diagnosing a 34% drop-off) and routing to the right agents. More persuasive than abstract copy — it answers "what does this do for me?" without a single feature bullet.
Role-Specific Social Proof
Avatar strip (GD / PM / QA / DS) directly signals the audience. Satisfies the "I am a…" recognition pattern — enterprise buyers self-identify immediately and feel the product is built for them.
Light Mode = Trust Signal
Enterprise procurement reviews often happen in office environments on standard monitors. Light mode signals maturity, reliability, and professionalism. Amber accent strip on Barista says "primary" without aggression — a minimum-change principle in action.
Best for: Enterprise sales conversations, B2B studio pitches, due diligence decks. The Barista mockup is a strong conversion tool — it lets buyers simulate the product before signing up. Recommend A/B testing this hero against Option 1's widget.
